Serene Japanese Pagoda Tutorial

Video by: Creator | Guide by: BuildItApp Team

Note: Video builds are generally more advanced. Take your time, pause the video frequently, and refer to the steps below.

Build Overview

Construct an elegant multi-tiered Japanese Pagoda with iconic curved roofs and intricate wooden details. This tutorial follows the video guide provided.

Difficulty: Intermediate-Advanced

Materials: Wood (Dark Oak, Spruce), Stone, Lanterns (Refer to video)

Build Phases (General Outline):

  1. Phase 1: Foundation & First Tier Base
  2. Phase 2: First Tier Walls & Roof
  3. Phase 3: Subsequent Tiers
  4. Phase 4: Top Spire & Details

Phase 1: Foundation & First Tier Base

Lay out the pagoda's base, establish the core pillar structure, and build the platform for the first main tier.

Step 1.1: Mark Out the Base Dimensions

Using your chosen primary block (e.g., Stone Bricks), mark out the square or rectangular footprint of the pagoda on flat ground as shown in the video.

Step 1.2: Construct Main Support Pillars

Erect the main vertical support pillars (e.g., Dark Oak Logs) at the corners and other key structural points identified in the video guide.

Step 1.3: Lay the First Floor Platform

Fill in the area between the pillars to create the solid floor for the first tier of the pagoda, typically using wooden planks.

Phase 2: First Tier Walls & Roof

This phase covers building the walls for the first tier and constructing its elaborate, curved roof.

Step 2.1: Build First Tier Walls

Connect the pillars with wall sections, incorporating any window or door designs shown in the video guide. Use appropriate wood types for these walls.

Step 2.2: Create the First Roof Eaves and Framing

Begin constructing the overhanging eaves of the roof. This often involves intricate placement of slabs and stairs to create the initial framing for the curve.

Step 2.3: Complete the First Tier Roof Surface

Carefully follow the video instructions to fill in the roof surface, achieving the signature upward curve of the pagoda roof using stairs, slabs, and full blocks of contrasting colors if desired.

Phase 3: Subsequent Tiers

Repeat the process for the second, third, and any additional tiers. Each upper tier is typically a smaller version of the one below it, maintaining the pagoda's tapering profile.

Step 3.1: Build Second Tier Platform and Walls

Construct the base for the second tier on top of the first roof, then build its walls, ensuring it's centered and scaled appropriately.

Step 3.2: Construct Second Tier Roof

Build the roof for the second tier, following the same principles as the first but on a smaller scale.

Step 3.3: Repeat for Additional Tiers

If the design has more tiers, continue this process, making each subsequent tier and its roof smaller.

Phase 4: Top Spire & Details

Complete the pagoda by constructing the topmost spire (finial) and adding decorative details such as lanterns, railings, banners, and any interior touches shown.

Step 4.1: Construct the Spire (Finial)

Build the intricate spire that crowns the pagoda, following the video's design. This often involves detailed work with fences, walls, and unique blocks.

Step 4.2: Add Decorative Elements

Place lanterns for lighting, add railings to balconies or edges, hang banners, and include any other traditional Japanese architectural details shown in the video.

Epic Medieval Castle Tutorial

Video by: Creator | Guide by: BuildItApp Team

Note: Video builds are generally more advanced. Take your time, pause the video frequently, and refer to the steps below.

Build Overview

Construct a grand stone fortress with towering turrets, strong walls, and a majestic keep. Follow the video guide for this quintessential fantasy build.

Difficulty: Advanced

Materials: Stone Bricks, Cobblestone, Wood, Banners (Refer to video)

Build Phases (General Outline):

  1. Phase 1: Layout & Foundation Walls
  2. Phase 2: Towers & Gatehouse
  3. Phase 3: Keep & Interior Structures
  4. Phase 4: Battlements & Details

Phase 1: Layout & Foundation Walls

Plan the castle's footprint and erect the main curtain walls.

Step 1.1: Define Castle Perimeter

Mark out the overall shape of your castle walls on the ground using a distinct block. Consider the terrain.

Step 1.2: Build Foundation of Curtain Walls

Construct the base layer of the main outer walls, ensuring they are thick and sturdy-looking.

Step 1.3: Raise Curtain Walls to Height

Build up the curtain walls to their desired height, varying the texture with different stone types if shown.

Phase 2: Towers & Gatehouse

Construct the defensive towers at corners and along walls, and build the main gatehouse.

Step 2.1: Build Corner Towers

Erect tall, robust towers at each major corner of your castle walls. These can be round or square.

Step 2.2: Construct Intermediate Wall Towers

If the walls are long, add smaller towers or bastions along their length for added defense and visual interest.

Step 2.3: Build the Main Gatehouse

Create a strong, defensible gatehouse, often with double towers, a portcullis area, and murder holes if detailed.

Phase 3: Keep & Interior Structures

Build the central keep (main tower/living quarters) and any other buildings within the castle courtyard like barracks, stables, or a great hall.

Step 3.1: Construct the Keep Foundation

Lay out and build the foundation for the castle's main keep, the most prominent and defensible structure.

Step 3.2: Build the Keep Walls and Floors

Raise the keep to its full height, adding multiple floors and internal divisions as shown in the video.

Step 3.3: Add Other Courtyard Buildings

If applicable, build any smaller structures within the castle walls according to the video guide.

Phase 4: Battlements & Details

Add battlements (crenellations) to walls and towers, flags, banners, and other fine details to complete the medieval look.

Step 4.1: Add Battlements and Crenellations

Create the classic notched tops on all walls and towers for archers and defense.

Step 4.2: Decorate with Banners and Flags

Design and place banners representing the castle's faction or lord.

Step 4.3: Final Touches

Add lighting, pathways, and any other small details shown in the video to bring the castle to life.

Mark out the overall dimensions of the mansion on a flat area using blocks like concrete or quartz.

Construct the floating island or magical anchor point and begin the tower's lower sections.

Automated Redstone Super Smelter Tutorial

Video by: Creator | Guide by: BuildItApp Team

Note: Video builds are generally more advanced, especially Redstone. Take your time, pause the video frequently, and refer to the steps below.

Build Overview

Build a highly efficient, multi-furnace Redstone contraption to smelt items quickly. Follow the video for this technical build.

Difficulty: Intermediate (Redstone knowledge helpful)

Materials: Furnaces, Hoppers, Chests, Redstone Dust, Comparators, Repeaters (Refer to video)

Build Phases (General Outline):

  1. Phase 1: Furnace Array & Item Input System
  2. Phase 2: Fuel Distribution System
  3. Phase 3: Output Collection System
  4. Phase 4: Control Circuit & Housing (Optional)

Phase 1: Furnace Array & Item Input System

Set up the bank of furnaces and the hopper system to feed items into the top of each furnace.

Step 1.1: Arrange Furnaces and Input Chest

Place your furnaces in a line or array. Position an input chest where you'll drop items to be smelted.

Step 1.2: Connect Hoppers for Item Input

Run a line of hoppers from the input chest, feeding into the top of each furnace in the array.

Phase 2: Fuel Distribution System

Create the hopper lines and any necessary Redstone to distribute fuel (coal, wood, etc.) to the back or side of each furnace.

Step 2.1: Place Fuel Input Chest

Position a chest where you will add fuel for the smelter.

Step 2.2: Connect Hoppers for Fuel Input

Run a line of hoppers from the fuel chest, feeding into the fuel slot (typically side or back) of each furnace.

Step 2.3: Implement Fuel Locking/Distribution (if shown)

Some designs use Redstone (comparators, repeaters) to ensure even fuel distribution or to lock hoppers when furnaces are full. Follow video closely.

Phase 3: Output Collection System

Build hopper lines underneath the furnaces to collect the smelted items and channel them into storage chests.

Step 3.1: Connect Hoppers for Output

Place hoppers underneath each furnace to draw out the smelted items.

Step 3.2: Channel Output to Collection Chests

Run the output hopper line(s) into one or more large chests for collecting the finished products.

Phase 4: Control Circuit & Housing (Optional)

Add any on/off switches or control mechanisms if shown in the video, and optionally build a neat housing around the smelter.

Step 4.1: Implement On/Off Switch (if applicable)

If the design includes a master switch (often using a lever and Redstone torches/repeaters to lock hoppers), build this circuit.

Step 4.2: Build Decorative Housing

Optionally, encase the Redstone contraption in a neat building or structure for aesthetics.

Steampunk Airship Tutorial

Note: Video builds are generally more advanced. Take your time, pause the video frequently, and refer to the steps below.

Build Overview

Construct a magnificent flying vessel with Victorian aesthetics and industrial machinery. Follow the video guide for this imaginative build.

Difficulty: Advanced

Materials: Wood, Copper, Iron, Wool (for balloon), Chains (Refer to video)

Build Phases (General Outline):

  1. Phase 1: Keel, Hull & Deck Construction
  2. Phase 2: Balloon/Envelope Structure & Rigging
  3. Phase 3: Propulsion Systems (Engines, Propellers)
  4. Phase 4: Gondola/Cabins & Steampunk Details

Phase 1: Keel, Hull & Deck Construction

Lay down the keel, build up the main body (hull) of the airship, and construct the main deck.

Step 1.1: Form the Keel and Basic Hull Shape

Create the foundational structure (keel) and the overall shape of the airship's main body, often using wood and metal blocks.

Step 1.2: Build Up the Hull Sides

Construct the sides of the hull, adding curves and details as shown in the video.

Step 1.3: Construct the Main Deck

Lay down the planking for the main deck of the airship.

Phase 2: Balloon/Envelope Structure & Rigging

Construct the large balloon or gas envelope that provides lift, and add any supporting rigging or framework.

Step 2.1: Create the Balloon Framework (if any)

Some designs have an internal frame for the balloon; build this first if applicable.

Step 2.2: Build the Balloon Envelope

Use wool or other lightweight-looking blocks to create the large, buoyant envelope above the hull.

Step 2.3: Add Rigging and Supports

Connect the balloon to the hull with chains, ropes (fences), or other support structures.

Phase 3: Propulsion Systems (Engines, Propellers)

Build the engines, propellers, and any steering mechanisms like rudders or fins, giving them a steampunk look.

Step 3.1: Construct Engine Housings

Build the structures that will house the airship's engines, often using metal blocks and pipes.

Step 3.2: Add Propellers or Other Thrusters

Create the propellers or other means of propulsion, detailing them with appropriate materials.

Step 3.3: Build Steering Mechanisms

Add rudders, fins, or directional thrusters for steering the airship.

Phase 4: Gondola/Cabins & Steampunk Details

Construct the gondola (passenger/crew area), cabins, and add numerous steampunk aesthetic details like pipes, gears, vents, and ornate railings.

Step 4.1: Build the Gondola or Main Cabin

Create the primary enclosed space for crew and passengers, often suspended below the hull or integrated into it.

Step 4.2: Add Steampunk Details (Pipes, Gears, Vents)

Decorate the airship extensively with copper pipes, iron gears, smokestacks, vents, and other industrial-Victorian elements.

Step 4.3: Final Touches (Railings, Lights, Interior)

Add safety railings, lighting, and any interior details for the cabins or command deck.

Cozy Underground Hobbit Hole Tutorial

Video by: Creator | Guide by: BuildItApp Team

Note: Video builds are generally more advanced. Take your time, pause the video frequently, and refer to the steps below.

Build Overview

Create a charming and rustic dwelling built into a hillside, with round doors and a warm, inviting interior. Follow the video guide.

Difficulty: Intermediate

Materials: Wood, Dirt, Stone, Flowers, Lanterns (Refer to video)

Build Phases (General Outline):

  1. Phase 1: Excavation & Exterior Shape Definition
  2. Phase 2: Round Doorway & Window Construction
  3. Phase 3: Interior Layout, Rooms & Basic Furnishing
  4. Phase 4: Exterior Landscaping, Garden & Finishing Touches

Phase 1: Excavation & Exterior Shape Definition

Find a suitable hillside and excavate the basic shape of the hobbit hole, defining its round frontal appearance.

Step 1.1: Choose Location and Excavate Initial Area

Select a hill or create one. Dig out the main area for your hobbit hole, creating a recess in the hillside.

Step 1.2: Shape the Frontal Facade

Use dirt, grass, and other natural blocks to shape the characteristic rounded front of the hobbit hole, blending it into the hill.

Phase 2: Round Doorway & Window Construction

Carefully construct the iconic round doorway and any round windows, a key feature of hobbit architecture.

Step 2.1: Build the Round Door Frame

Use wood planks, stairs, and slabs to create the circular frame for the main door.

Step 2.2: Install the Door

Place the actual door within the frame. This might be a custom design using multiple door parts or trapdoors if shown.

Step 2.3: Create Round Windows

Similarly, build circular frames for any windows, often using glass panes and wooden elements.

Phase 3: Interior Layout, Rooms & Basic Furnishing

Define the interior rooms (living area, kitchen, bedroom) and add cozy, rustic furniture. Hobbit holes are known for their warm, cluttered charm.

Step 3.1: Define Interior Room Layout

Excavate further into the hill to create space for different rooms. Use wooden beams or supports if desired.

Step 3.2: Furnish the Living Area

Add a fireplace, comfortable seating (stairs and slabs), bookshelves, and other homey touches.

Step 3.3: Create a Cozy Kitchen and Bedroom

Furnish the kitchen with barrels, a crafting table, and a furnace. Make the bedroom snug with a bed and chests.

Phase 4: Exterior Landscaping, Garden & Finishing Touches

Blend the hobbit hole into the landscape with dirt, grass, flowers, a small garden, a chimney, and other charming details.

Step 4.1: Add Exterior Greenery and Flowers

Cover the roof and surrounding area with grass blocks, bone meal for tall grass, and plant colorful flowers.

Step 4.2: Create a Small Garden or Path

Add a quaint garden patch with crops, or a winding path leading to the door.

Step 4.3: Build a Chimney and Add Final Details

Construct a rustic chimney (often from brick or stone) and add lanterns or other exterior lighting.

Futuristic Science Base Tutorial

Video by: Creator | Guide by: BuildItApp Team

Note: Video builds are generally more advanced. Take your time, pause the video frequently, and refer to the steps below.

Build Overview

Construct a high-tech, futuristic science research facility, complete with laboratories, advanced technology displays, and a sleek, modern aesthetic.

Difficulty: Advanced

Materials: Concrete (White, Gray, Cyan), Glass (Stained), Iron Blocks, Sea Lanterns, Quartz (Refer to video)

Build Phases (General Outline):

  1. Phase 1: Main Structure & Foundation
  2. Phase 2: Laboratory Modules & Corridors
  3. Phase 3: Central Hub & Advanced Tech Displays
  4. Phase 4: Exterior Details & Lighting
  5. Phase 5: Interior Outfitting & Final Touches

Phase 1: Main Structure & Foundation

Lay out the foundational footprint of the science base and construct the primary structural elements.

Step 1.1: Define Base Footprint

Mark out the overall area for the science base using light-colored concrete or quartz blocks.

Step 1.2: Build Main Structural Supports

Erect the primary support pillars or foundational walls that will define the base's main sections.

Step 1.3: Construct Base Flooring

Lay down the initial flooring for the main levels, often using smooth, clean blocks like polished andesite or gray concrete.

Phase 2: Laboratory Modules & Corridors

Build individual laboratory modules or sections and connect them with sleek, futuristic corridors.

Step 2.1: Construct Outer Walls of Lab Modules

Build the exterior walls of the first lab module, often incorporating large glass panels or specific colored concrete accents.

Step 2.2: Add Roofing to Modules

Complete the roof of the lab modules, maintaining a flat, modern aesthetic.

Step 2.3: Build Connecting Corridors

Create enclosed corridors or walkways to link different modules, often using glass and smooth concrete.

Phase 3: Central Hub & Advanced Tech Displays

Develop a central hub or command center and incorporate elements that look like advanced technology or research equipment.

Step 3.1: Build the Central Hub Structure

If the design includes a main operations center or a larger central room, construct this area.

Step 3.2: Create "Tech" Displays

Use blocks like iron, beacons, stained glass, item frames with maps, or armor stands to simulate computers, machinery, or research projects.

Phase 4: Exterior Details & Lighting

Add exterior features like antennas, sensor arrays, landing pads, and implement futuristic lighting solutions.

Step 4.1: Add Exterior Antennas or Equipment

Place decorative structures on the roof or sides that resemble scientific equipment like antennas or solar panels.

Step 4.2: Implement Exterior Lighting

Use sea lanterns, glowstone, or shroomlights concealed or integrated into the structure for a clean, futuristic glow.

Phase 5: Interior Outfitting & Final Touches

Furnish the laboratories with workstations, storage, and specific research setups. Add final details throughout the base.

Step 5.1: Outfit Laboratories

Add tables (slabs/stairs), chests, item frames as monitors, and other blocks to represent lab equipment.

Step 5.2: Add Interior Lighting and Details

Ensure all interior spaces are well-lit with a modern feel. Add details like custom doors (pistons if applicable), warning signs, or color-coded pathways.

Step 5.3: Final Walkthrough and Refinements

Check the entire base for consistency, add any missing details from the video, and ensure a polished final look.
